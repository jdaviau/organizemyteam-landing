Member reports help you see payment status at the family level—who's paid, who owes, and the details of each account. These reports are essential for managing collections and communicating with families.

## Payment Status Report

The core member report shows payment status for all families:

### Generating the Report

1. Go to **Reports → Member Status**
2. Select options:
   - All members or filtered
   - Include payment details or summary only
   - Sort by name, balance, or status
3. Click **"Generate"**

### Report Contents

For each family:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Name** | Family/member name |
| **Status** | Paid, Partial, Overdue |
| **Total Due** | Sum of all charges |
| **Total Paid** | Sum of all payments |
| **Balance** | Amount still owed |
| **Last Payment** | Date of most recent payment |

### Filtering Options

View specific groups:
- **All members**: Complete roster
- **Paid in full**: Balance = $0
- **Partial payment**: Paid something, balance remaining
- **Nothing paid**: No payments recorded
- **Overdue**: Past due date, balance remaining

## Individual Member Statements

Detailed view of one family's account:

### Generating a Statement

1. Go to **Team → Members**
2. Click on family name
3. Click **"View Statement"** or **"Generate Statement"**

### Statement Contents

Complete account history:

```
ACCOUNT STATEMENT
Family: Johnson Family
Generated: March 15, 2025

CHARGES
Date        Description              Amount
Feb 1       Registration Dues        $150.00
Mar 1       Tournament Fee           $50.00
                         Total Due: $200.00

PAYMENTS
Date        Description              Amount
Feb 15      Check #1234              $150.00
                        Total Paid: $150.00

                   BALANCE DUE: $50.00
```

### Sending Statements

Email statements to families:
1. Generate statement
2. Click **"Email to Family"**
3. Optional: Add personal note
4. Send

Useful for:
- Families questioning balance
- Formal payment requests
- End-of-season reconciliation

## Exporting Member Data

### Export Options

- **PDF**: Professional statement format
- **CSV**: Raw data for spreadsheets
- **Excel**: Formatted with totals

### What's Included

Exports contain:
- Family contact information
- All charges and payments
- Current balances
- Payment history

### Privacy Note

Member data includes personal information. Handle exports carefully:
- Don't share publicly
- Delete when no longer needed
- Follow your organization's privacy policy

## Using Reports for Collections

### Identify Priority Accounts

1. Generate overdue report
2. Sort by balance (highest first)
3. Focus on largest outstanding amounts

### Track Follow-Up

After contacting families:
1. Add notes to their account
2. Record when you reached out
3. Note any arrangements made

### Measure Progress

Compare reports week-over-week:
- How many accounts moved to paid?
- Did total outstanding decrease?
- Which reminders are working?

## Communicating with Families

### Balance Inquiry Response

When a family asks "what do I owe?":

1. Pull their statement
2. Show all charges and payments
3. Point to current balance
4. Offer to review any questions

### Dispute Resolution

If family believes balance is wrong:

1. Generate detailed statement
2. Review each transaction together
3. Look for missing payments
4. Verify against their records
5. Correct if error found

### Payment Confirmation

When payment is recorded:
1. Statement updates automatically
2. Family can view online (if enabled)
3. Send confirmation email (optional)

## Report Scheduling

### Automated Reports

Set up regular reports:

1. Go to **Reports → Scheduled**
2. Create schedule for member status
3. Choose frequency (weekly recommended)
4. Report emails to you automatically

### Why Schedule?

- Never forget to check status
- Consistent monitoring
- Early identification of issues
- Historical record

## Best Practices

### Weekly Review

Every week, review:
- Who moved to paid this week?
- Who's newly overdue?
- Any accounts needing attention?

### Clean Data

Reports are only as good as the data:
- Record payments promptly
- Keep contact info current
- Update charges when they change

### Consistent Follow-Up

Use reports to drive action:
- Overdue list → send reminders
- Large balances → personal contact
- Paid in full → thank you

### Document Everything

Keep records of:
- Reports generated
- Communications sent
- Arrangements made
- Payments promised

## Troubleshooting

### Family not appearing

1. Verify they're added to team
2. Check spelling of name
3. Ensure not filtered out
4. Confirm charges assigned

### Balance seems wrong

1. Generate detailed statement
2. Verify all charges applied
3. Check all payments recorded
4. Look for duplicates or errors

### Can't send statement

1. Verify email address on file
2. Check email isn't bouncing
3. Try generating PDF and sending manually
4. Confirm email settings in system
